## 📁 Projects

### 🔗 Project 1 — Supply Chain & Finance Analytics

**Files:** `Project-2(Supply_Chain_Mega_Project_Using_SQL).sql` · `.pdf` · `Summary .pdf`

#### Business Problem
A manufacturing business needed to understand how sales channels, pricing tiers, and promotional discounts were impacting revenue flow — but lacked structured analysis across its supply chain data.

#### Analysis Performed
- Sales channel comparison (direct vs distributor vs retail)
- Invoice pricing and pre-invoice deduction analysis
- Promotional discount impact on net revenue
- Distribution channel efficiency insights
- Revenue flow breakdown by segment and region

#### SQL Concepts Used
`JOINs` `GROUP BY` `Subqueries` `Aggregate functions` `CTEs` `Window functions` `CASE statements`

#### Key Insights
- Pre-invoice deductions significantly impact net revenue across certain channels
- Promotional discounts vary widely by product segment, affecting margin consistency
- Distribution channel analysis reveals opportunities to reduce cost-to-serve

### 🔗 Project 2 — Customer Behavior Analysis

**Files:** `project-3(Case Study - Exploring Customer Behavior).sql` · `.pdf` · `Presentation.pdf`

#### Business Problem
The business needed to understand which customers were driving revenue, how purchase patterns varied over time, and where to focus retention efforts.

#### Analysis Performed
- Customer purchase pattern analysis
- Revenue contribution by customer segment
- Top customers identified by revenue and frequency
- Monthly revenue trend visualisation
- Product affinity and repeat purchase analysis

#### SQL Concepts Used
`GROUP BY` `ORDER BY` `RANK()` `DENSE_RANK()` `Window functions` `Date functions` `Subqueries` `Aggregations`

#### Key Insights
- A small group of customers accounts for a disproportionate share of total revenue
- Monthly trends show clear seasonal revenue cycles
- High-frequency, low-value customers behave differently from low-frequency, high-value ones
